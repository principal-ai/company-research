# LFX Venture Partners

**LFX Venture Partners** (LFX Ventures) is a global venture capital firm investing
in "the future of supply chain and logistics" — cutting-edge technology and
disruptive business models across Manufacturing, Logistics, and Commerce
Enablement. It is the venture arm of the **Li & Fung Group** (founded 2021 as a
rebrand of Fung Capital). Our contact is **Heather Lee** (Investor).

## Overview

- **Firm:** LFX Venture Partners / LFX Ventures — venture arm of the Li & Fung Group; founded 2021 (rebrand of Fung Capital). Offices in Los Angeles and Silicon Valley/San Francisco.
- **Stage:** Early to growth — third-party profiles list pre-seed, seed, Seed+/extension, Series A–C, and growth. Card comfort: Traction, Revenue; rounds Seed, Series A.
- **Sectors:** Manufacturing (concept→production), Logistics (B2B transportation), Commerce Enablement (delivery & commerce infrastructure).
- **Geography / teams:** Global — card lists "Global / Agnostic" (no US-only constraint).
- **Style:** Open to both (leading and following), per card.
- **Check size:** Not disclosed by LFX.
- **Leadership:** General Partners **John Seung** and **Janie Yu**; Investment Committee includes **Ed Lam** and **Spencer Fung** (Li & Fung).
- **Portfolio (selected):** Pipe17, Shippeo, UniUni, GoFreight, Fleetworks, invent.ai, Cimulate, Worldly. Exits include Narvar and GT Nexus.

## Thesis & what they value

- **"We know global commerce / global value chains."** The firm invests in technology reshaping how goods move, transactions happen, and supply chains operate, with a stated view that the upstream supply chain is "largely manual" and prime for disruption.
- **Manufacturing, Logistics, Commerce Enablement.** Three pillars from concept-to-production through delivery/commerce infrastructure.
- **AI × global commerce; agentic + infrastructure layers (contact's focus).** Per our card, Heather Lee's work "sits at the intersection of AI and global commerce with a particular focus on the agentic and infrastructure layers." This is the angle that touches Principal — recorded here as the contact's stated focus, not a fund-wide thesis statement.
